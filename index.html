The Berry Ratio in Transfer Pricing: Concept, Logic and Practical Use

In transfer pricing analysis, transactions between associated enterprises must satisfy the Arm’s Length Principle. To evaluate whether the pricing between related parties is comparable to that between independent enterprises, various transfer pricing methods and financial indicators are used.

The Berry Ratio is a Profit Level Indicator commonly applied in cases involving distribution or intermediary functions. The ratio measures the relationship between gross profit and operating expenses and evaluates whether a distributor earns an appropriate return relative to the services it performs.

Berry Ratio = Gross Profit / Operating Expenses

The concept originated in the landmark U.S. case E.I. DuPont de Nemours & Co. v. United States. This metric is particularly relevant for limited-risk distributors or service providers where operating expenses represent the primary driver of value creation.
