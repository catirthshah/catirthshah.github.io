The Berry Ratio in Transfer Pricing

In transfer pricing analysis, transactions between associated enterprises must satisfy the Arm’s Length Principle. One financial indicator sometimes used in benchmarking distribution functions is the Berry Ratio.

The Berry Ratio measures the relationship between gross profit and operating expenses. It evaluates whether a distributor earns a reasonable return relative to the functions it performs such as marketing, sales and distribution.

The concept originated in the landmark U.S. case E.I. DuPont de Nemours & Co. v. United States where the profitability of a Swiss distributor was analysed by comparing its gross profit to its operating expenses instead of relying solely on product margins.

Berry Ratio = Gross Profit / Operating Expenses

The ratio is particularly relevant for limited-risk distributors because operating expenses represent the primary driver of value creation. However its reliability depends on proper classification between cost of goods sold and operating expenses as well as comparability with independent companies.
