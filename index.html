The Berry Ratio in Transfer Pricing

The Berry Ratio is a financial indicator used in transfer pricing analysis to determine whether a distributor earns an appropriate return relative to the operating expenses incurred.

Berry Ratio = Gross Profit / Operating Expenses

The concept originated in the landmark case of E.I. DuPont de Nemours & Co. v. United States. It is particularly useful in analysing limited-risk distributors where operating expenses represent the primary value-creating activities.

However the reliability of the Berry Ratio depends on proper classification between cost of goods sold and operating expenses and comparability with independent enterprises.
