The Practitioner

Tirth Shah,
Chartered Accountant

A qualified CA and member of ICAI with a focused practice built at the intersection of tax law, judicial precedent, and strategic advisory. My work demands more than mechanical compliance — it requires rigorous legal research, current knowledge of judicial trends, and a deep understanding of each client's economic reality.

A significant focus of my practice is advising NRI clients and high-value businesses on optimizing their tax structures through lawful planning, treaty analysis, and informed litigation strategy — appearing before tax authorities across income tax and GST proceedings, and advising on complex cross-border transactions including Form 15CA/15CB certification.

I have developed particular depth in Transfer Pricing, especially the application of financial metrics like the Berry Ratio under TNMM, and regularly write technical articles to make complex tax law accessible to practitioners and businesses.

The Berry Ratio in Transfer Pricing: Concept, Logic and Practical Use

In transfer pricing analysis, transactions between associated enterprises must satisfy the Arm's Length Principle. The Berry Ratio is a Profit Level Indicator applied in cases involving distribution or intermediary functions, measuring the relationship between gross profit and operating expenses.
