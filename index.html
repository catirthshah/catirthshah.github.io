The Berry Ratio in Transfer Pricing: Concept, Logic and Practical Use

In transfer pricing analysis, transactions between associated enterprises must satisfy the Arm’s Length Principle. To evaluate whether the pricing between related parties is comparable to that between independent enterprises, various transfer pricing methods and financial indicators are used.

The Berry Ratio is a Profit Level Indicator commonly applied in cases involving distribution or intermediary functions. It evaluates whether a distributor earns an appropriate return relative to its operating expenses.

Berry Ratio = Gross Profit / Operating Expenses

This metric evaluates how much profit is generated for each unit of operating cost incurred. It is particularly relevant for limited-risk distributors where operating expenses drive value creation.
