The Berry Ratio in Transfer Pricing

The Berry Ratio is a financial indicator used in transfer pricing analysis to evaluate whether a distributor earns an appropriate return relative to its operating expenses.

The ratio is calculated as:

Berry Ratio = Gross Profit / Operating Expenses

It is particularly relevant for limited-risk distributors and intermediary entities where operating expenses represent the primary driver of value creation.

The concept was developed in the landmark DuPont case and later recognised in transfer pricing guidance including the U.S. Section 482 regulations and OECD Transfer Pricing Guidelines.
