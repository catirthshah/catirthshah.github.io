Articles

The Berry Ratio in Transfer Pricing

The Berry Ratio is a financial indicator used in transfer pricing analysis to evaluate whether a distributor earns an appropriate return relative to the operating expenses incurred in performing distribution functions.

Berry Ratio = Gross Profit / Operating Expenses

The ratio is particularly relevant for limited-risk distributors where operating expenses represent the primary driver of value creation.

The concept originated in the DuPont case and is discussed in transfer pricing literature including OECD guidance and U.S. Section 482 regulations.
