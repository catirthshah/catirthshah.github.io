The Berry Ratio in Transfer Pricing

The Berry Ratio is a Profit Level Indicator commonly applied in cases involving distribution or intermediary functions. It evaluates whether a distributor earns an appropriate return relative to the services it performs.

Berry Ratio = Gross Profit / Operating Expenses

This metric evaluations how much profit is generated for each unit of operating cost incurred, originating from the landmark U.S. case E.I. DuPont de Nemours & Co. v. United States.
