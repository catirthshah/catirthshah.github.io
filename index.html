The Berry Ratio in Transfer Pricing: Concept, Logic and Practical Use

In transfer pricing analysis, transactions between associated enterprises must satisfy the Arm's Length Principle. To evaluate whether pricing between related parties mirrors what independent parties would agree upon, practitioners employ Profit Level Indicators (PLIs).

The Berry Ratio is one such PLI — applied in cases involving distribution or intermediary functions. It measures the relationship between gross profit and operating expenses to assess whether a limited-risk distributor earns a return proportionate to the services it provides.

Berry Ratio = Gross Profit ÷ Operating Expenses

Developed from the landmark U.S. case E.I. DuPont de Nemours & Co. v. United States, the ratio has since gained recognition under OECD Transfer Pricing Guidelines and U.S. Treasury Regulations.
